NB: please consider that some students may have technical issues outside of their control that impact their virtual presentations. If a student has a slow system or service outage during their presentation, they should be marked on the basis of what they were able to deliver

## Content 

This section gauges the depth of understanding and clarity of information presented about the technical project. It assesses the student's knowledge level, technical understanding, and the coherence of information.

| **Score** | **Caption**                           | **Details**                                                                           |
| --------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | Did not present                       | Student did not present.                                                              |
| 1         | Poor understanding of the project     | Incoherent presentation with no clear structure or technical detail.                  |
| 2         | Limited understanding of the project  | Presentation lacks clarity, organisation, and technical detail.                       |
| 3         | Adequate understanding of the project | Some clarity and organisation in the presentation, but lacking any technical detail.  |
| 4         | Good understanding of the project     | Clear and organised presentation with sufficient technical detail.                    |
| 5         | Thorough understanding of the project | Clear, concise, and well-organised presentation. Excellent depth of technical detail. |

### Tips 
- Assess how well the student comprehends the technical project. Look for evidence of in-depth research and knowledge.
- Evaluate the clarity and coherence of the information presented. Check if complex concepts are explained clearly and concisely.
- Determine if the student covers technical aspects adequately without oversimplification or excessive complexity.

## Organisation and Structure

This section evaluates the logical flow and structure of the presentation, including the introduction, main points, transitions between topics, and the coherence of the conclusion.

| **Score** | **Caption**                          | **Details**                                                                                                 |
| --------- | ------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | Did not present                      | Student did not present.                                                                                    |
| 1         | Poor organisation and structure      | No apparent organisation. Presentation lacks a clear structure.                                             |
| 2         | Limited organisation and structure   | Disorganised structure with unclear transitions between topics.                                             |
| 3         | Adequate organisation and structure  | Adequate organisation with an introduction, main points, and conclusion, but transitions are lacking.       |
| 4         | Good organisation and structure      | Mostly logical flow with a clear introduction, main points, and conclusion. Some transitions may be abrupt. |
| 5         | Excellent organisation and structure | Logical flow with a clear introduction, main points, and conclusion. Smooth transitions between topics.     |

### Tips 
  - Evaluate the introduction's ability to set the stage for the presentation and clearly outline what will be covered.
  - Look for a logical progression of ideas and a well-structured sequence of topics. Assess the transitions between sections.
  - Assess the conclusion for summarising key points and providing a clear takeaway message.

## Q&A Handling 

This section assesses how well the student responds to questions, including the accuracy, depth, and clarity of their answers. It measures their ability to engage in a discussion about the presented material.

| **Score** | **Caption**                          | **Details**                                                                                                                                               |
| --------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | No Q&A                               | Did not answer or was not asked any questions.                                                                                                            |
| 1         | Poor question handling               | Unable to handle questions effectively. Responses lack clarity.                                                                                           |
| 2         | Difficulty handling questions        | Provided inaccurate or incomplete responses.                                                                                                              |
| 3         | Adequate handling of questions       | Adequate handling of questions but may struggle with some responses. Limited depth of knowledge in certain areas.                                         |
| 4         | Mostly handles questions confidently | Appropriate depth of knowledge. Mostly handles questions confidently with mostly clear and accurate responses. Some areas may need further clarification. |
| 5         | Handles questions with confidence    | Demonstrates deep knowledge of the project. Handles questions with confidence and provides clear, accurate responses.                                     |

### Tips 
- Assess how clearly and effectively the student responds to questions asked, ensuring accuracy and depth in explanations.
- Look for the student's ability to engage in a constructive discussion, encourage further inquiry, and expand on presented concepts.

## Time Management

This section evaluates the student's ability to adhere to the allocated presentation time, maintain a balanced pace throughout the presentation, and effectively manage time for each section without rushing or dragging.

| **Score** | **Caption**                    | **Details**                                                                                             |
| --------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | Did not present                | Student did not present.                                                                                |
| 1         | No evidence of time management | Presentation significantly exceeds or falls short of the allotted time.                                 |
| 2         | Poor time management           | Poor time management, resulting in a rushed or extended presentation.                                   |
| 3         | Adequate time management       | Adequate time management, but the presentation may be too short or too long. Uneven pacing.             |
| 4         | Good time management           | Mostly fits within the allotted time. Some sections may be rushed or prolonged.                         |
| 5         | Excellent time management      | Presentation fits within the allotted time. Well-paced with appropriate time allocated to each section. |

### Tips 
- Evaluate whether the presentation fits within the allotted time frame without rushing or dragging.
- Assess if each section of the presentation is given an appropriate amount of time without causing imbalance.

## Overall Delivery and Engagement

This section focuses on the student's manner of presenting, including engagement, confidence, pace of speech, and overall ability to keep the audience interested and attentive. It measures the overall impression the presentation leaves on the audience, including the student's ability to captivate, inspire interest, and create a memorable and impactful experience for the listeners.                 

| **Score** | **Caption**                            | **Details**                                                                                                               |
| --------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0         | Did not present                        | Student did not present.                                                                                                  |
| 1         | Ineffective delivery                   | Fails to engage the audience effectively, lacks pace, constant distractions. Navigates challenges or hiccups poorly       |
| 2         | Delivery is awkward or distracting     | Limited audience engagement, and inconsistent pace.                                                                       |
| 3         | Adequate delivery                      | Adequate engagement with the audience, reasonable pacing with only one or two distractions. Limited challenges or hiccups |
| 4         | Mostly engaging and confident delivery | Maintains a reasonable pace. Mostly captivates the audience, with some elements fostering interest and engagement.        |
| 5         | Engaging and confident delivery        | Appropriate pace, and varied tone. Captivates the audience, fostering interest in a smooth impactful presentation.        |

### Tips 
- Evaluate the ability of the presentation to capture and maintain the audience's attention throughout.
- Consider if the presentation leaves a lasting impression, engages the audience, and inspires further interest in the topic.
- Assess the student's ability to engage the audience through enthusiasm, passion, and confidence.
- Evaluate the pace of speech (not too fast or slow) and variations in tone to maintain audience interest.
